Title: Employee Onboarding Handbook

Welcome to Lumina Manufacturing. New hires spend their first week on payroll setup, laptop enrollment, badge access, and mandatory security training. Employees are expected to complete acceptable-use training before requesting production dashboards, and managers must approve any travel booked less than fourteen days before departure. Reimbursement for meals follows the India domestic policy unless the traveler is assigned to a customer workshop in Singapore.

During orientation, customer-facing teams are introduced to the product stack in broad terms rather than implementation detail. The Orion Sensor is installed on factory equipment and captures vibration and temperature telemetry. That telemetry moves through the Atlas Gateway before it appears in Nimbus Analytics, which is the application most account teams use when customers ask about uptime trends, sensor drift, or late deliveries. New solution engineers are told not to promise custom dashboards during week one; they should first learn the standard operational views already configured in Nimbus Analytics.

Plant visits require steel-toe shoes, and first-time visitors to Chennai should coordinate local transport through workplace services rather than using personal ride claims. For access control, contractors cannot enter the Pune demo lab without an escort, even if they hold a valid corporate badge. The onboarding team also reminds engineers that customer escalation contacts differ by region and that launch-week support windows are stricter for automotive accounts than for internal training environments.

Employees frequently ask whether all platform components are managed by the same operations group. The short answer is no. Platform Reliability owns Atlas Gateway firmware stability and the operational health of Nimbus Analytics, while field teams are responsible for installation quality at the customer site. If a new hire joins support, they are expected to memorize the telemetry path from Orion Sensor to Atlas Gateway to Nimbus Analytics before they shadow live incidents.
