Title: Q3 Launch Readiness Brief

The Q3 launch package for automotive accounts combines Orion Sensor, Atlas Gateway, and Nimbus Analytics. The sales team prefers to describe this as a single operating system for plant visibility, but internally the launch office tracks each component separately because the dependencies are not identical. Pune is scheduled before Singapore because local implementation partners in Pune have already completed training and site-readiness checks.

The launch office does not want every team escalating normal readiness issues into the executive review. Even so, two dependencies remain non-negotiable. BlueRiver Circuits must maintain controller board output for Orion Sensor, and Delta Batteries must return to normal shipment volume for Atlas Gateway. If either supplier slips, the launch sequence can still look healthy in a slide deck while being operationally fragile in reality.

Customer success requested a standard executive dashboard in Nimbus Analytics so rollout reviews stop relying on manually assembled spreadsheets. The required dashboard elements are gateway health, sensor drift, and supplier delay signals. Legal also asked the team to remove older references to experimental pilot regions because only Pune and Singapore are approved for the Q3 announcement.

New account executives reading this brief should ignore earlier drafts that mentioned open-market expansion. The current plan is narrower by design. It prioritizes reliable delivery over geographic spread, and it assumes that any Chennai routing risk will be managed through established continuity procedures rather than ad hoc exceptions.
